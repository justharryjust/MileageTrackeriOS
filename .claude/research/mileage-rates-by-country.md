# Mileage Rates by Country — Research Reference

Generated by the Rates Agent research run (see [`.claude/agents/rates-agent.md`](../agents/rates-agent.md)). This is the sourced audit trail — see [`country-rate-expansion.swift.txt`](country-rate-expansion.swift.txt) for the corresponding staged Swift.

## ⚠️ Stale data found in the app

Two jurisdictions already hardcoded in `officialRates` were found to be out of date:

- **United Kingdom** — HMRC raised the Car/Van rate from 45p to 55p per mile, effective 6 April 2026 (the first AMAP change in 13+ years). HMRC also publishes a 20p/mile Bicycle rate that the app doesn't have at all.
- **New Zealand** — IRD's current 2025–26 rates (Petrol 120→37 c/km, Diesel 130→38, Petrol Hybrid 90→24, Electric 122→23, all tiered at 14,000 km) are materially different from the app's previous figures (104→34 combined Petrol/Hybrid/EV, 83→28 Diesel). IRD also publishes 4 distinct fuel-type rows, not the 2 the app had.

Australia was re-checked and confirmed correct and unchanged (88 c/km flat).

## 3. No official rate (confirmed absence)

Confirmed via primary sources that no government-published standard mileage rate exists. Do not populate these with a figure — market/industry norms are noted for context only and are explicitly not official.

| Country | ISO2 | Notes | Confidence | Source |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Singapore | SG | IRAS confirms no prescribed standard mileage rate exists — only guidance on the taxability of employer-paid reimbursements. Market practice (S$0.60–0.80/km) is informal, not government-sanctioned. | High confidence in the negative | [IRAS](https://www.iras.gov.sg/taxes/individual-income-tax/employers/understanding-the-tax-treatment/transport) |
| Japan | JP | National Tax Agency has no per-km business mileage rate. A separate, unrelated NTA commuting-allowance cap table exists (monthly yen ceiling by one-way commute distance band) — must not be conflated with a business-mileage rate. | Medium confidence in the negative (not an exhaustive crawl of every NTA circular) | [NTA](https://www.nta.go.jp/taxes/shiraberu/taxanswer/gensen/2585.htm) |
| India | IN | Income Tax Department (§10(14)/Rule 2BB) has no per-km rate. Commuting conveyance allowance is a flat monthly exempt amount (₹1,600/month); business-travel reimbursement exemption is actual-expense-based, no prescribed per-km schedule. Market benchmarks (₹3–5/km two-wheelers, ₹9–14/km cars) are informal industry norms. | High confidence in the negative | [Income Tax India](https://www.incometaxindia.gov.in/w/rule-2bb) |
